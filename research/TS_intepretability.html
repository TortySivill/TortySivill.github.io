Interpretability For Time Series In Health



Interpretability dominates discourse surrounding the development of trustworthy Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems which are of particular demand in high-stakes domains such as medicine. Progress in interpretability for time series data, however, is relatively limited when compared to image or natural language processing. Time series classification has enormous potential in health data science yet a lack of understanding of temporal properties has hindered its widespread adoption. This paper applies interpretability techniques to time series at the data analysis stage to facilitate data understanding, feature engineering and classification. We motivate the need for powerful aggregate features that capture the temporal trends of time series data and propose three novel complexity measures that outperform existing approaches when used as classification features. We evaluate our proposed methodology on the Depresjon dataset, an imbalanced, noisy dataset recording activity levels in depressed and non-depressed individuals. Our proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the Depresjon dataset, exemplifying how interpretability at the data analysis stage is capable of improving classification performance as well as data understanding.
