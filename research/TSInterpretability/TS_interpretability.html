Routine is Important: Complexity Measures for Non-Stationary Time Series Classification in Health

Project develops complexity measures for non-stationary time series. Paper submitted to MLHC 2021 - paper link coming soon!

Paper Abstract: The digitization of medical records has led to the proliferation of extensive time series datasets that have the potential to unlock insight into biological processes, the effectiveness of treatments and the progression of disease. This paper explores how the non-stationarity of time series data makes classifying them a challenge. Through a case study of activity level data and its association with depression, we argue that most biological processes are not only inherently non-stationary, but it is that very non-stationarity that makes them interesting. Complexity measures calculate the changeability of a time series and have proved beneficial for their classification. However, we show how existing complexity measures fail to differentiate between non-stationary time series and propose three novel complexity measures which capture non-stationarity, outperforming existing measures when evaluated on the classification of a variety of time series datasets of varying characteristics. Interpretability dominates discourse surrounding the development of trustworthy Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems which are in particular demand in healthcare. This paper demonstrates that interpretability does not have to be compromised for accuracy by leveraging the power of inherently interpretable models when used alongside a carefully selected set of features.
